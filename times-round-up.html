Times round-up of problematic candidates

June 8, 2024
far-right islamophobia misc racism

The Times has published a round-up of several problematic candidates, shortly after the deadline for General Election selection passed. Of the Reform candidates, The Times reported this:

NameConstituencyRemarks
Robert LomasBarnsley NorthClaimed asylum seekers had it “in their DNA to lie” and that “black people of Britain” were “grifting the race card”
Marc BurcaKensington and BayswaterDefended Ghislaine Maxwell, saying her sentence was “very harsh and vindictive” as “she seemingly facilitated a few 16-17-year-old prostitutes”
Hugo MillerHorshamCalled black Americans “negroes” and said they should feel “gratitude” for being allowed to integrate into American society
Andrea WhiteheadLeeds West and PudseyLiked a Facebook post labelling Sadiq Khan an “undercover Jihadist”
Craig BirtwistleWorsley and EcclesLiked a post calling for a complete ban on Islam, which added: “Let them leave if they don’t like it.”
Ken FergusonWirral WestLiked an anti-vax message on social media, an Islamophobic tweet joking that Muslim men married 12-year-olds and a post denying human-induced climate change
Garry SutherlandExmouth and Exeter EastFound guilty and fined £2,000 for kicking a dog in August 2023
Stewart SutherlandBlaenau Gwent and RhymneyPromoted a tweet by leader of Britain First that claimed Sunak and Khan’s political success was evidence of the “Great Replacement”, a racist conspiracy theory
Lucian FernandoHastings and RyeFined and disqualified from driving in 2021 for being drunk in charge of a car, and six months later was caught driving whilst disqualified and also had a charge upheld for failing to provide a blood sample. “I made a mistake and I learnt from it.”
