Steve Beatty adds Conspiracy Theorist to his resumé

June 20, 2024
conspiracy

It shouldn’t surprise anyone that Didcot and Wantage candidate Steve Beatty, previously linked to Gary Raikes on Facebook, turns out to be a conspiracy theorist. The Express reported to have commented “Correct” on a cartoon of the Bible’s three wise men, with one saying “there was no pandemic”, another saying “climate change is a hoax”, and a third saying “Men can’t get pregnant”:

In another post, Beatty joined in the WEF conspiracy favoured by some Reform supporters, suggesting that Rishi Sunak and Keir Starmer “represent” their “paymasters”, the World Economic Forum.

He further suggested that Keir Starmer is being “installed” as Prime Minister to “take the country to war and implement Agenda 2030”.

‘Agenda 2030’, a meeting of the UN to set Sustainable Development Goals, is linked by some conspiracy theorists to numerous nefarious plans, such as forcing people to eat insects, and confining them in ‘15 minute cities’.

Despite standing for election in the General Election, he also confusingly described the “whole ‘election’” as a “farce”.
