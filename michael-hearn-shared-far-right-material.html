Michael Hearn accused of sharing 'fascist' material

June 29, 2024
far-right immigration islamophobia racism

Writing for Byline Times, Olly Haynes has revealed troubling content shared by Richmond Park candidate Michael Hearn.

The article reveals that Hearn previously faced calls to resign from the Conservative Party after sharing far-right material. In 2018, as reported by Evolve Politics, Hearn had listed Hajo Hermann as an ‘inspirational person’ on his Facebook account.

Hajo Hermann was a Nazi lawyer who went on to defend Holocaust deniers (including David Irving) and neo-Nazis in court late into his life.

Hearn is also reported to have shared content from neo-fascist group Generation Identity, and Britain First.

Calling for people to vote for Reform on Facebook recently, Hearn is reported to have written “as an Englishman we have seen our entire country changed without our consent without thought without consideration to our tribes within the kingdom our heritage and culture”, and “rapists murderers terrorists and tomorrow’s groomers of our children can just walk in”.

Hearn appears to have at least two Twitter/X identities (@StormHearn and @HearnStorm), both of which combine parts of his full name, and are linked by references to the East India Company logo, references to Stevenage, and being a paramedic. Both of these accounts have posted racist content. For example, in a reply to a post referring to London Mayor Sadiq Khan walking his dog, @HearnStorm posted

Has he strapped small explosive vests to them yet like his friends in Hamas do.
