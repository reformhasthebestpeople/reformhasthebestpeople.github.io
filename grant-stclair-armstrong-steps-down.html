Grant StClair-Armstrong steps down over BNP links

June 17, 2024
far-right

The Times reports that Grant StClair-Armstrong has stepped down as Reform candidate for North West Essex.

In comments unearthed from a 2010 blog, StClair-Armstrong encouraged voters to vote for the British National Party. He wrote “I could weep now, every time I pick up a British newspaper and read the latest about the state of the UK. No doubt, Enoch Powell would be doing the same if he was alive. My solution … vote BNP!”

The comments were found on an archived version of the website, which has since been changed.

When approached by The Times about the comments, StClair-Armstrong said: “I don’t have this stuff on my website any more. … I removed all that stuff and made the website respectable. … I cleaned up my act a long long time ago. I’m sure if you look around you’ll find some really nice posts, how I feel about animals, animal welfare, how I feel about the planet. It’s not all bad.”

Regarding the Enoch Powel and BNP comments, he said “I’ve got no excuses for that. I think they’re a disgusting party. I don’t like the English Defence League. I don’t like them.” He added that he saw no alternative but to stand down as a Reform candidate.
